  \begin{psection}{The Problem}
    Relocalization (the ``kidnapped robot'') asks for an accurate camera pose in
    a \emph{known} environment. Transformer-based SfM such as VGGT [1] returns
    fast feed-forward poses, but \textbf{cannot use such a prior}, and degrades
    when views are few and barely overlap. We add a \textbf{super-quadric} scene
    prior [4].
  \end{psection}

  \begin{psection}{The Environment Prior}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/superquadric_family.png}\\[-0.2ex]
    \cpt{A super-quadric is one shape family. An exponent sets its roundness,
      three half-extents set its size, and a pose places it, so a handful tile a room.}
    \vskip0.7ex
    \raggedright
    \begin{minipage}[c]{0.49\linewidth}
      SuperDec [4] fits a compact set of \textbf{super-quadrics} to the scene
      point cloud (right). During BA, a one-sided point-to-quadric term pulls
      each triangulated point toward its nearest primitive, with EM-style
      re-association every few iterations:
    \end{minipage}\hfill
    \begin{minipage}[c]{0.47\linewidth}
      \centering
      \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/superquadrics_3d_clean.png}\\[-0.3ex]
      \cpt{Fig.\ 1: scene as 189 super-quadrics (floor hidden) + cameras.}
    \end{minipage}
    \vskip0.7ex
    {\large
    \[
      E=\underbrace{\sum_{i,j}\rho\!\left(\lVert \pi(R_i,t_i,X_j)-x_{ij}\rVert^2\right)}_{\text{reprojection}}
       +\underbrace{\sum_{j}\Big(\lambda\,\lVert q_j\rVert\cdot\max\!\big(0,\,1-F(q_j)^{-\varepsilon_1/2}\big)\Big)^2}_{\text{one-sided surface prior}}
    \]}
    \vskip-0.2ex
    {\small where $F(\cdot)$ is the inside-outside function of the super-quadric
    nearest to point $X_j$ and $\lambda$ the prior weight [5].}
  \end{psection}

  \begin{psection}{Setup}
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Dataset:} Aria Synthetic Environments (10 scenes)
      \item \textbf{Models:} VGGT for feed-forward poses (via MapAnything [2]),
            MASt3R for matching [3]; SuperDec [4] for the prior
      \item \textbf{Optimization:} full triangulation + surface BA in Ceres;
            \textbf{metric:} pose AUC@5 (higher is better)
    \end{itemize}
  \end{psection}

  \flexgap

  \begin{psection}{Results}
    Three stages: raw VGGT poses, \textbf{bundle adjustment}, then BA $+$
    \textbf{our super-quadric prior}. BA does most of the work (raw VGGT averages
    only $9.3$ AUC@5 at 10 views, $20.4$ at 6). The prior then adds a gain
    \emph{on top of BA} that is largest when views are few.

  \flexgap

  \begin{psection}{Conclusion}
    At high view counts, dense correspondences already constrain the poses, so the
    prior adds little ($+0.2$ at 10 views). As views drop and co-visibility
    collapses, reprojection alone under-constrains the geometry. The super-quadric
    surface supplies the missing constraint, recovering up to $+2.0$ AUC@5 at 4
    views with light $\lambda$ tuning. \textbf{An environment prior encoded as a
    super-quadric cloud helps relocalization most under sparse, low co-visibility
    views.}
  \end{psection}
