  \begin{psection}{The Problem}
    In relocalization (the ``kidnapped robot''), camera pose must be recovered
    accurately \emph{given} a known environment. Transformer-based SfM (e.g.\
    VGGT [1]) gives fast, strong feed-forward poses but \textbf{cannot exploit
    such a prior} --- and it degrades when views are few and barely overlap.
    We bridge this gap with a \textbf{super-quadric} scene prior [4].
  \end{psection}

  \begin{psection}{The Environment Prior}
    \begin{minipage}[c]{0.56\linewidth}
      SuperDec [4] fits a compact set of \textbf{super-quadrics} to the scene
      point cloud. During BA, each triangulated point is pulled onto its nearest
      primitive by a one-sided point-to-quadric term, re-associated EM-style
      every few iterations:
    \end{minipage}\hfill
    \begin{minipage}[c]{0.40\linewidth}
      \centering
      \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/superquadrics_3d_clean.png}
    \end{minipage}
    \vskip0.4ex
    \cpt{Figure 1: Super-quadric decomposition of a scene (189 primitives) with camera poses.}
    \vskip0.6ex
    {\large
    \[
      E=\underbrace{\sum_{i,j}\rho\!\left(\lVert \pi(R_i,t_i,X_j)-x_{ij}\rVert^2\right)}_{\text{reprojection}}
       +\underbrace{\sum_{j}\Big(\lambda\,\lVert q_j\rVert\cdot\max\!\big(0,\,1-F(q_j)^{-\varepsilon_1}\big)\Big)^2}_{\text{one-sided surface prior}}
    \]}
    \vskip-0.2ex
    {\small where $F(\cdot)$ is the inside--outside function of the super-quadric
    nearest to point $X_j$ and $\lambda$ the prior weight [5].}
  \end{psection}

  \flexgap

  \begin{psection}{Setup}

      \item \textbf{Dataset:} Aria Synthetic Environments (10 scenes)
      \item \textbf{Models:} VGGT for feed-forward poses (via MapAnything [2]),

      \item \textbf{Optimization:} full triangulation + surface BA in Ceres

  \flexgap

  \begin{psection}{Conclusion}
    At high view counts dense correspondences already constrain the poses, so the
    prior is largely redundant ($+0.2$ at 10 views). As views drop and
    co-visibility collapses, reprojection alone under-constrains the geometry ---
    and the super-quadric surface fills exactly that gap, recovering up to
    $+2.0$ AUC@5 at 4 views. \textbf{Encoding an environment prior as a
    super-quadric cloud helps relocalization most where it matters: sparse,
    low-covisibility views.}
  \end{psection}
